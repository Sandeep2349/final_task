Rituals

  1. Bhogi: Burning old household items in a bonfire (Bhogi Mantalu) to symbolize spiritual cleansing and renewal.
  2. Makara Sankranti: Celebrating the main festival with decorative floor art (Muggulu), prayers to the Sun God, and festive feasts.
  3. Kanuma: Worshipping and decorating cattle to express gratitude for their vital role in agricultural prosperity.
  4. Mukkanuma: Offering final prayers to natural elements and gathering with family for traditional non-vegetarian celebrations.

Traditional Foods

  • Sweets:
    • Tilgul: Sesame seeds and jaggery sweets symbolizing warmth and energy.
    • Ariselu: Sweet rice flour fritters made with jaggery syrup.
    • Pongal: A savory dish made from rice and lentils, often garnished with ghee, nuts, and spices.
  • Snacks:
    • Chakralu: Crunchy spiral-shaped snacks made from rice flour.
    • Murmura Laddu: Sweet balls made from puffed rice and jaggery.
  • Sugarcane: Fresh sugarcane stalks are commonly consumed during the festival as a symbol of sweetness and prosperity.
  • Ariselu: A classic deep-fried sweet made from a dough of newly harvested rice flour and molten jaggery.
  • Chakkara Pongali: A rich, sweet rice pudding cooked with moong dal, jaggery, and generous amounts of ghee and nuts.
  • Boorelu: Deep-fried golden dumplings stuffed with a sweet mixture of Bengal gram (senaga pappu) and jaggery.
  • Sunnundalu: Nutritious, roasted urad dal laddoos bound together with pure ghee and jaggery or sugar.
  • Pulihora: A tangy and spicy tamarind-based rice dish that serves as a savoury contrast to the festival's sweets.
  • Garelu: Crispy, deep-fried lentil donuts (vada) traditionally enjoyed with ginger chutney or spicy chicken curry.
